Final Defense

Air Pressure Multi-Purpose Event Cannon

Hudson Tien, Castro Valley High School, Engineering Design and Development

Opening: This project pivoted from a spring-loaded cannon to an air pressure cannon because the final design needed to be safer, more adjustable, and more realistic to build.

Problem

Events Need Distance Without Complexity

CVHS needs a safe, reliable launcher that can send school event items farther than a normal throw without requiring Leadership or event staff to understand complicated equipment.

Stakeholders

Designed Around Actual Users

  • Leadership class needs fast setup and simple operation.
  • Students need shirts and confetti to reach more of the crowd.
  • Coaches and event organizers need safety and predictability.

Research

Existing Solutions Were Incomplete

Hand throwing is cheap but inconsistent and limited by fatigue.

Commercial launchers work well but are expensive for a school project.

The old school cannon was not used because setup was not intuitive.

The spring concept required high forces and custom manufacturing.

Pivot

Why the Design Changed

The spring-loaded concept looked simple, but the force and manufacturing requirements made it a poor fit. Regulated air pressure made the design adjustable, testable, and easier to explain to non-engineering users.

Final Solution

Prototype Subsystems

  • Air chamber or tank stores launch energy.
  • Gauge and valves let the user fill, check, and release pressure.
  • Launch tube guides the payload.
  • Frame, labels, and checklist support safe operation.

STEM

Pressure Creates Launch Force

F = P x A

Air pressure applies force across the barrel area. More pressure generally creates more launch force, but the safe operating range is limited by the weakest rated component.

Requirements

Measured Against User Needs

  • Learn operation in under 2 minutes.
  • Reload in under 60 seconds.
  • Reach at least 60 feet with a standard shirt bundle.
  • Show pressure before launch.
  • Moveable by one or two students.

Testing

Test Plan

  • Range test at multiple pressure levels.
  • Reload time test with repeated launches.
  • Usability test with a first-time user.
  • Leak and safety review before operation.

Evaluation

What Reviewers Should Confirm

  • Pressure limits and labels are clear.
  • Operation is easier than the old cannon.
  • Frame and mounts are strong enough for repeated use.
  • Testing data supports the final claims.

Reflection

The First Idea Was Not the Best Final Design

The project showed that engineering is not protecting the original concept. It is changing the concept when evidence shows a better path.

Next Steps

Recommended Improvements

  • Add permanent brackets and a transport case.
  • Attach a printed checklist to the frame.
  • Test more payload shapes and masses.
  • Add a fixed angle indicator for repeatability.

Conclusion

Useful Means Usable

The final air pressure prototype better solves the event problem because it balances launch performance with simple operation, safety checks, and repeatable testing.
